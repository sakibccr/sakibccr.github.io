> Begin at once to live, and count each separate day as a separate life.
>
> <cite>— Seneca</cite>

I work at a medium-sized company that builds software. I am part of a small team of seven people. I don't see much motivation in my team, and I am rarely enthusiastic about the work I do. Whenever I get a chance to kill some time, I take it. My co-workers do the same. Days go by, and my guilt and sadness about the uselessness of my time stack up.

The reason behind this isn't the usual suspects like a poor work environment or poor pay. Nor is it the work ethic of my co-workers. It's something else.

I intend to find out exactly what it is that creates motivation or the lack thereof. It could be personal or something within the organization.

One thing comes to mind, though. Our organization doesn't have many bad things, but the problem is, it doesn't have a lot of good things either. It maintains a _good enough_ standard for everything. In other words, it does the bare minimum for the employees. Much like matters in interpersonal relationships, the bare minimum doesn't work. To foster motivation and enthusiasm among workers, you need to create a _better-than-good-enough_ environment.

But of course, there could be, and probably are, many other factors.
