Eshi’s cat passed away three days ago in an unfortunate accident involving a motor vehicle. He was rushed to the hospital but, sadly, couldn’t be saved.

Eshi is understandably devastated.

Before its death, the cat must have gone through a lot of pain. But what was the purpose of this pain? In fact, what is the purpose of pain in general?

From an evolutionary perspective, pain is a defense mechanism. Without it, no animal would try to protect itself, and life might have died out long before the emergence of any life form capable of asking such questions.

So, pain, it turns out, has value. But in this case, the cat died despite enduring it. The pain didn’t help save its life. For the cat, it was nothing but pain, with no reward in the end. That feels unfair.

Then again, the laws of nature don’t account for fairness. Even if the cat had survived after enduring the pain, what difference would it have made—to the cat itself, to other cats, to humans, or to the universe? Ultimately, perhaps, none. It’s all meaningless.

Rest in peace, Tufan. Eshi's world is a little less without you.
